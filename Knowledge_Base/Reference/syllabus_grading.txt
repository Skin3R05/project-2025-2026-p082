Course syllabus and grading rules (assessment criteria and percentages) at IPB.

Every course unit (UC) has a Ficha de Unidade Curricular (FUC) — the official course sheet. It lists the syllabus (program/contents), the learning outcomes, the teaching hours and, importantly, the assessment method with the exact weight of each component (for example: final exam 60%, practical project 30%, attendance/participation 10%). The exact percentages are decided by each course unit and written in its FUC.

Where to find the syllabus and grading percentages
- ECTS information guide (course catalogue with the syllabus and assessment of every UC, for all schools and programmes): https://portal3.ipb.pt/index.php/pt/guiaects
- In the ECTS guide, open your school, then your programme, then the course unit — the assessment section shows the components and their percentages.
- The grading and assessment rules in general are set by the academic regulations (Regulamento de Frequência e Avaliação): https://webdocs.ipb.pt/portal/download?docId=31612

Grading scale: IPB grades go from 0 to 20; 10 is the minimum pass mark.

If a student asks for the exact percentages of a specific subject and it is not in the documents, point them to that course unit's sheet in the ECTS guide above, or to the secretariat of the school that teaches it.
