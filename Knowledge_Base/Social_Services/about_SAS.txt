Message from the Administrator

Dear students,

The Administrator of the Social Action Services (SAS) of IPB would like to remind you that the Social Services do not only support those facing economic difficulties. Do not hesitate to bring any problems related to academic life to the Social Services.

The SOCIAL ACTION SERVICES OF THE POLYTECHNIC INSTITUTE OF BRAGANÇA (SAS-IPB) is an organisation that provides services to all students of the schools that make up this Polytechnic Institute.

More specifically, they are responsible for food in canteens and bars, residences, scholarships, culture, sport, medical support, and social-psychological support, with the aim of improving the conditions of those who study at the Polytechnic Institute, especially those most in need.

These Services were created by Decree-Law 129/93 of 4 April by the Portuguese Government, granting them financial and administrative autonomy.
The head office of the Social Action Services is on the campus of the Polytechnic Institute at Quinta de Santa Apolónia / Av. Sá Carneiro; tel. +351 273 303361; 4;5;7;8 Fax +351 273 331557, in the centre of the city of Bragança, and is served by public transport.

Services provided by the SAS of IPB

Scholarships – The SAS award scholarships to students of the Institute, and the amount depends on each student's economic situation.

Every year, those interested must submit an application online on the SAS website, by filling in a form and uploading scanned documents over the internet. Declarations must be justified. The award is based on criteria defined by the government, taking into account the economic situation, academic success and other factors.

The benefits granted are a monthly, non-repayable cash amount. Residence, transport, and internship subsidies, among others, may also be granted.

In addition to the scholarships granted by the SAS, students may obtain scholarships from the Calouste Gulbenkian Foundation, the Regional Governments of Madeira and the Azores, Municipal Councils and other Foundations.

Residences

For students who live away from home, the SAS have rooms in their own residences equipped with kitchens, study rooms, laundry, telephone, TV, Internet, photocopier, etc. They are assigned according to the scholarship amount, cases considered very urgent, and the distance from the parents' home.

The cost of the room is deducted from the scholarship.

At the SAS, students can also find directions to accommodation in private houses or shared with colleagues.

Food

The SAS of IPB have a Central Canteen for the entire IPB Campus and a Bar in each School. The Central Canteen is managed directly by the SAS and consists of several rooms with a diverse offer of meals at different prices. The student card must be presented to access the Canteen. Meals can also be had at the bars.

Some students who wish to may work a few part-time hours in the Canteen, Residences, or carry out other services as collaborators in exchange for payment.

Culture and Sport

The Social Action Services of IPB are twinned with the Studentenwerk of Munich (Germany) and with the Azienda Regionale per il Diritto allo Studio Universitario of Florence (Italy), with the aim of promoting student mobility, cultural exchanges and the organisation of study visits for students from these countries. These cultural contacts are made on a reciprocal basis and do not entail any cost for participants except for travel.

The Student Associations find in the SAS an important ally for support in organising common festivities such as "Semana Académica", "Semana do Caloiro", "Queima das Fitas", "Magustos", etc.

The Male and Female Tunas are associations of great visibility both in the country and abroad, of which the Polytechnic Institute is very proud.

Sporting events, theatre, music and journalism are areas of special interest that receive particular attention from the SAS through direct or indirect financial support.

Medical and social-psychological support

In March 2007 a Health Office began operating, with consultations in the Psychological and Social field. This Office is made up of four Psychologists, one Social Worker, two nurses and two dieticians. Support covers the areas of Psychology, Social work, Family Planning, Drug Addiction and dietetics. These specific types of support extend to anyone who requests them, provided they have proven material needs, and they are free of charge.
